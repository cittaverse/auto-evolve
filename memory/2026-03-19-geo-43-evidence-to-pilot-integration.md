## Evidence Synthesis: Usability & Acceptance Factors for Digital RT

### Yang 2026 — Qualitative Study (PMC12953960)
**Title**: "Perspectives of older adults and healthcare providers on digital technologies in reminiscence therapy"  
**Source**: SAGE Digital Health, Mar 2026  
**Method**: Qualitative (focus groups/interviews)  
**Participants**: Older adults + healthcare providers

**Key Themes** (from search snippets):
- **Barriers**: Technology anxiety, privacy concerns, perceived complexity, lack of personalization
- **Facilitators**: Social connection support, meaningful content, ease of use, caregiver involvement
- **Critical insight**: Both older adults AND providers emphasize **relationship-preserving** technology (not replacing human connection)

### IET 2026 — Prospective Usability Study
**Title**: "Informing the Development of Reminiscence Technology for Older Adults"  
**Source**: IET Healthcare Technology Letters, Feb 21 2026  
**Method**: Prospective study of digital reminiscence platform  
**Focus**: Psychosocial + usability factors influencing acceptance

**Key Factors** (from search snippets):
- **Psychosocial**: Perceived relevance to identity, emotional safety, autonomy support
- **Usability**: Interface simplicity, clear feedback, error tolerance, accessibility (vision/hearing)

### Technology Acceptance Models for Older Adults (2025-2026 evidence)

| Model | Key Constructs | Relevance to CittaVerse |
|-------|---------------|------------------------|
| **TAM** (classic) | Perceived Usefulness, Perceived Ease of Use | Baseline, but insufficient for older adults |
| **UTAUT** | Performance Expectancy, Effort Expectancy, Social Influence, Facilitating Conditions | Better, includes social factors |
| **STAM** (Senior TAM) | TAM + Health Status, Anxiety, Self-Efficacy, Support | ⭐ Most relevant — designed for older adults |
| **Extended TAM** (2025 studies) | TAM + Trust + Privacy Concerns + Personalization | ⭐⭐ Critical for AI+RT context |

### SUS Benchmarking (Digital Health Apps)
- **Standard SUS**: 10 items, score 0-100, benchmark = 68 (average)
- **Simplified SUS** (for cognitively impaired/older adults): Same 10 items, simplified language
- **Recommendation**: Use Simplified SUS for Pilot (validated for older adults)

## Recommended Additions to Day 1-4 Evaluation

### 1. Pre-Session Baseline (Day 1, before intervention)

**Technology Anxiety Scale** (adapted from STAM, 5 items, 5-point Likert):
1. "I feel nervous using new technology"
2. "I worry I will make mistakes when using this system"
3. "I feel confident I can learn to use this tool" (reverse)
4. "Technology makes me feel overwhelmed"
5. "I am comfortable trying new digital tools" (reverse)

**Privacy Concerns** (3 items, 5-point Likert):
1. "I am concerned about how my personal stories will be stored"
2. "I trust the system to keep my information private" (reverse)
3. "I am comfortable sharing memories with an AI system"

### 2. Post-Session Measures (Days 1, 2, 3, 4)

**Perceived Personalization** (4 items, 5-point Likert):
1. "The questions felt relevant to my life experiences"
2. "The system understood what I was sharing"
3. "The conversation felt personalized, not generic"
4. "I felt the AI was genuinely interested in my stories"

**Emotional Safety** (4 items, 5-point Likert):
1. "I felt emotionally safe during this session"
2. "I could share difficult memories without feeling pressured"
3. "The system responded appropriately to my emotions"
4. "I felt in control of what I shared"

**Social Influence** (2 items, 5-point Likert):
1. "My family/caregivers think I should use this tool"
2. "I would recommend this to other older adults like me"

### 3. Caregiver Feedback (Days 4, 7, 14)

**Caregiver Observation Form** (brief, 5 items):
1. "My family member seemed engaged during sessions" (1-5)
2. "I noticed positive mood changes after sessions" (1-5)
3. "The system was easy for my family member to use" (1-5)
4. "I felt involved in the process appropriately" (1-5)
5. "I would recommend this tool to other families" (1-5)

**Open-ended**:
- "What did you notice about your family member during/after sessions?"
- "What concerns or suggestions do you have?"

### 4. Simplified SUS (Post-Day 4, or Day 7 follow-up)

Use the **Simplified SUS for Older Adults** (10 items, standard scoring):
1. I think I would like to use this system frequently.
2. I found the system unnecessarily complex. (reverse)
3. I thought the system was easy to use.
4. I think that I would need the support of a technical person to be able to use this system. (reverse)
5. I found the various functions in this system were well integrated.
6. I thought there was too much inconsistency in this system. (reverse)
7. I would imagine that most people would learn to use this system very quickly.
8. I found the system very cumbersome/awkward to use. (reverse)
9. I felt very confident using the system.
10. I needed to learn a lot of things before I could get going with this system. (reverse)

**Scoring**: 0-100, benchmark ≥68 for "acceptable"

## Confidence & Uncertainty

**High confidence**:
- STAM/Extended TAM constructs are validated for older adults
- Simplified SUS is appropriate for MCI/older adult populations
- Caregiver feedback is critical (Ni 2026 JAMDA evidence)

**Medium confidence**:
- Specific item wording may need Chinese cultural adaptation
- Optimal timing of assessments (pre/post/follow-up) may need iteration

**Low confidence**:
- Exact effect sizes of these additions on pilot outcomes
- Response burden impact on older adult participants

---

## Implications for Pilot

1. **Evaluation burden increases** from ~6 min to ~13 min post-session (Day 1), ~8 min (Days 2-3), ~10 min (Day 4)
   - **Mitigation**: Split assessments (immediate: 4 items; Day 7 follow-up: full SUS + caregiver)
   
2. **Caregiver involvement required** — need to collect caregiver contact info during screening

3. **Cultural adaptation needed** — all scales should be translated + back-translated for Chinese context

4. **Data analysis complexity increases** — need to plan for:
   - Pre-post changes in anxiety/personalization/safety
   - Correlation between usability scores and narrative quality scores
   - Caregiver vs. participant perspective alignment
