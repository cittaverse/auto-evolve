## Cross-Synthesis: What This Means for CittaVerse

### Bottom Line
The AI+RT systematic review landscape is still nascent — Shankar 2025 is only a protocol, Seo 2025 is a scoping preprint. **No completed, published systematic review of AI in RT exists as of March 2026.** This is both a gap and an opportunity.

### Key Findings

1. **Digital RT > Traditional RT** (Pu 2025, network meta-analysis): Our digital-first approach is evidence-backed as the most effective delivery format
2. **Dual-stakeholder effects matter** (Ni 2026, JAMDA): RT benefits both patients and caregivers — validates our B2B2C model targeting both
3. **User acceptance factors identified** (Yang 2026, IET 2026): Psychosocial + usability factors are critical — directly applicable to Pilot Day 1-4 evaluation
4. **GenAI validates RT enhancement** (RemVerse 2025, Kimono 2025): AI-generated content (VR scenes, culturally relevant images) effectively triggers and deepens reminiscence
5. **Cultural sensitivity is critical** (Kimono 2025): Japan-context findings emphasize cultural adaptation — our Chinese cultural context needs similar attention
6. **No completed AI+RT systematic review yet** (Shankar protocol only): CittaVerse could contribute evidence to fill this gap

### Confidence Assessment
- **High confidence**: Digital RT effectiveness, AI-enhanced RT feasibility
- **Medium confidence**: Specific effect sizes for AI-enhanced vs standard RT (no head-to-head RCTs yet)
- **Low confidence**: Long-term maintenance effects of AI-enhanced RT (insufficient follow-up data)

### Implications for Pilot (Path B)
1. **Day 1-4 evaluation should include usability factors** from Yang 2026 and IET 2026 findings
2. **Caregiver outcomes should be tracked** even in product pilot (Ni 2026 evidence)
3. **Cultural sensitivity protocol needed** — not just translating Western RT, but incorporating Chinese cultural memory anchors (已在元记忆策略中部分实现)
4. **Positioning opportunity**: When Shankar's full review publishes, CittaVerse pilot data could be cited as one of the first AI+RT implementations with empirical evidence
